The Door

It was never meant to be a restaurant.

Avodah by Stellamoon began the way most good things in Singapore do — quietly, in a home, for friends. Chef Kent, classically trained across Cantonese and Japanese kitchens, started cooking for his wife Stella's circle. Word moved the way it always does here: one dinner, one photo, one friend telling another.

Years later, the address hasn't changed. The food has only gotten more exact. What guests keep saying — in their own words, unprompted, across more than a hundred reviews — is that it doesn't feel like a restaurant at all. It feels like being let in.

Knowing that I am a whisky lover, every time I go… Chef Kent and Stella made it personal. — Chen Jun Wen, Local Guide · returning guest

The House

Four things you won't find
at any restaurant.

Every private dining room tries to feel intimate. Ours actually is one — Stella and Kent's real home, with the things they've collected over a decade still on the shelves.

壁画

Stella's Art on Every Wall

The paintings aren't decor — they're hers. Guests linger over them after dessert more often than they check their phones.

威士忌

A Whisky Wall, Opened on Request

Kent will talk you through the collection if you let him — guests describe it as the most knowledgeable, least pretentious whisky conversation they've had in Singapore.

景色

A Balcony, A Skyline, No Crowd

Described by more than one guest as "a million-dollar view" — without the velvet rope or the markup.

遊び

The Claw Machine

Yes, really. A genuine arcade claw machine, fully stocked, because a dinner this good can still have a sense of humour.
